Internet of Medical Things Wearables

3 Feb 2022 5 Min read

Have you ever lost your phone and tracked it using Find My App or accessed your phone gallery using the drive. Having machines connected to the internet has brought us numerous advantages. Internet of things is all about connecting machinery, utensils and equipment to the internet, sharing data over cloud storage. IoMT is about internet-connected medical devices, hardware and software infrastructures used for multiple medical purposes. Wearable and remote devices would share the health data it gains over the internet to any location in real-time, beneficial for medical analysis. This gives birth to fields like bioinformatics that deals with an enormous number of data to figure out different patterns.

How does IoMT impact healthcare

In-home IoMT allows the patients to share their medical data and reports over the internet for relevant healthcare providers' further advice in real-time. Telehealth is a successful method for a virtual consultation with higher efficiency. This is a distance communication methodology with any medical Institute they prefer worldwide and prevent the hazard of visiting a doctor for mild health concerns. In-hospital IoMT has shown more significant progress and efficiency in the past couple of years. Tele-Surgeries, keeping track of patient movements, and emergency care are the major applications of in-hospital IoMT.

Evolution of healthcare wearables

Wearables used in healthcare runs back to the history of the 13th century, wearing spectacles for vision adjustment purposes. In the mid-twenties, Holter monitor, a portable electrocardiogram device is introduced to measure the irregular heartbeat. Followed by that, different wearable devices were created worldwide to serve multiple health check purposes. The advancement of technology has made the medical wearables more advanced and smaller in size, which is convenient enough to fit it to your hand like a watch or wear it as a replacement for your wedding ring.

Fitness Trackers and Smart Watches

A fitness tracker could be considered an important IoMT device out there in the market. According to a study, it shows 20% of the American population wear a smartwatch or a fit bit to track their fitness levels. These devices are directly connected to the mobile device via the internet and Bluetooth, and data is stored in cloud storage. These fitness data such as oxygen levels, blood pressure levels, heartbeat rate, calories burned, hours of sleep and number of steps would be a great set of data to be analyzed to diagnose different health conditions. Machine learning algorithms integrated into Fitbit apps would give a comprehensive idea of your health progress would alarm you when to exercise. Studies have shown people who uses Fitbit at the age of 40 to 60 has a low tendency of having issues like high blood pressure. In the recent events of Covid-19, we identified that having low oxygen levels as a symptom of Covid-19.

Apple smartwatch developers are currently on technologies where Apple watch would track users having irregular heartbeats and send them a notification mentioning the stats. Further, they are working on sensers that can detect erratic pulse, which can be a sign of atrial fibrillation (AFib), increasing the risk of blood clots, strokes, and other related complications.

Motion Tracker Wearables

Other than smartwatches and Fitbits, several wearables are created for designated purposes, such as measuring blood sugar levels, skin temperature levels. Further electrocardiogram measurements and electroencephalogram measurements are using smart wearables. Mostly measuring vital signs have been a crucial part of these devices.

Smart Clothing

Smart clothing has become a latest invention in this decade, which would give solutions for multiple issues. Smart wearables may be capable of tracking irregular heartbeats, but they cannot perform any action back on the body to regulate it. This is where Smart clothing comes into place. When you wear a LifeVest, it will track your heartbeat. If it detects any irregularity in the heartbeat, LifeVest is capable of giving a shock back on your body to restore it. This would prevent tragic incidents such as Sudden Cardiac Deaths, which happens when the heartbeat is too low to pump the required blood to the body or when the heart stops beating abruptly.

Further, smart clothing has been a solution for people with conditions like Parkinson's Tremor. These people. This is a condition where your body parts start shaking while still and mostly happens when sleeping or when the body doesn't perform any motion. Smart clothing can settle this issue by identifying those situations and reducing the shaking amplitude by 85%.

Placing sensors and relevant instruments in the suitable locations of cloth and connecting wires through clothing textiles so that the user doesn't feel uncomfortable are crucial when designing these wearables.

With technologies like quantum computing and nanotechnology, tons of medical devices would be available in the market for consumers. But using the suitable instruments made by maintaining the standard procedures would be an essential factor to consider when using these equipment in daily life.
