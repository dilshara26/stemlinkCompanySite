Gravitational Time Dilation and physics behind the Interstellar movie

20 Jan 2022 8 Min read

'Interstellar' has become one of the favorite movies of any Sci-Fi enthusiast. It's an astonishing creation done by the well-known film director Christopher Nolan who has also been the producer of mind-blowing movies like Tenet and Inception. Interstellar movie was framed around a space exploration mission beyond the galaxy searching for a planet suitable for a human territory. The movie includes tons of physical concepts such as gravitational time dilation, quantum entanglement, the singularity of a black hole, neutron stars, multi-dimensional realities, Bootstrap paradox and much more. Therefore when understanding the concept of time dilation, Interstellar would set a great visual foundation to understand it practically. Time dilation could be simply said as the slow down in the flow of time due to the effects of gravitational force or relative velocity. Before we dig in deep, time dilation was first discovered by sir Albert Einstein, and there are interesting stories that explain how he first got the idea to research this

Gravitational Time dilation

As you know, gravity is the force by which a planet or a particular body draws other objects towards its center and that's why all the planets are in orbit around the sun. The time dilation due to gravity could be explained using the space-time cloth. The time curvature is happening in the 4th dimension and since we cannot sense the behavior in the 4th dimension, we assume our space as two-dimensional and the depth as the 3rd dimension for explanation purposes. Space-time cloth is a hypothetical stretchable cloth. When you place an object with a lesser density, you could see a low curvature in the cloth, and if you place an object with a higher density, you could see a very high curvature in space-time cloth or in other words, a high curvature in time. The higher the curvature in time, the lesser the flow of time, and the lesser the curvature would cause the time to flow relatively fast. The gravitational pull of a planet or body acting on other objects towards it's centre depends on the radius and the mass of that planet or body. Higher the mass and lesser the radius of a particular body, the gravitational pull towards the center of that body would be high. When the gravitational force is high, the curvature of time would be increased, and the time flow would slow down.

According to the given equation, Universal Gravitational Constant and the speed of light stays as contents. So, when the mass and the radius of the planet increase and decrease, respectively, the time dilations would be high, or else time flow would slow down. The singularity in the black hole has an infinitely large mass and an infinitely small volume which would cause to have a compelling gravitational force towards its center for an extent where the flow of time would stop at the event horizon of the black hole.

Time Dilation in Interstellar

Christopher Nolan has successfully visualized this concept in two significant situations in the movie. When Cooper and his crew landed in the spaceship on the Millers' planet, they clearly state that Miller's planet has a 130% higher gravitational force than the earth, and 1 hour here would be seven years on earth. The reason for high gravitational force is that Miller's planet was closer to Gagentua Blackhole. As mentioned above, closer to black holes, there's a significant slow down in time due to very high gravitational force in a singularity. Before they landed the spaceship on Miller's planet, Cooper's daughter's age was around 14 years and Cooper's age was 35. But after spending just three hours on Miller's planet, when cooper took a call to his daughter, she said that this was her 35th birthday. This means for the 3 hours Cooper has spent on Miller's planet; his daughter has aged more than 21 years. This scenario vividly explains the extent time would slow down in greater gravity surfaces relative to earth.

In the second level of comparison, we could consider the situation where Romiley ages 23 years than the rest of the crew. Cooper realized that instead of orbiting around Miller's planet, he decided to take a wider orbit like endurance to conserve fuel and time. But they forgot to consider the time shift in the endurance and Miller's planet. At this point, Romiley decided to stay on board endurance to study the black hole and the rest of the crew landed on Miller's planet using a Ranger. The surprising fact was that, when the crew returns to the spaceship after spending close to 3 hours on Miller's planet, Romeily has spent 23 years on the spaceship waiting for the crew to return.

Here comes the mind-thriller part in the movie. First you get see Cooper falling in to the black hole and in the ending, you see him surviving. An object that falls inside a black hole would be horizontally crushed and vertically stretched by an infinitely large force, making the survival of cooper questionable. Different arguments stating that before he falls in, 5th-dimensional humans rescued him and sent him towards a Tesseract. Another group of people argues that he fell into the black hole, discovered the singularity characteristics, and sent those data backwards the time so that Murph could solve the equation of manipulating the gravity.

However, the most exciting part is that, even though cooper spent just several hours inside the black hole, after waking up in the Cooper station, he found his daughter has grown much older and is more than a hundred years old. Also, Cooper found himself 124 years old relative earth even though he has spent just a few months or weeks for the whole space journey relative to him after leaving the earth.
