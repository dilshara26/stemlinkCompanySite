Robotic trends in 2030

13 Jan 2022 3 Min read

Robotics has come a long way from Science-Fictions to Reality in the 21st century. Once, people believed robots were machines that looked like humans, as seen in the Transformers film series. Nevertheless, robotic appliances have been used in various industries for performing multiple tasks in both physical and software forms. In many early years, robots were just machines that performed a specific task such as transporting a set of goods or fixing parts of a car. But with the emerging of AI, how developers looked into robotics drastically changed, and AI-powered robots became much more powerful, safer and more efficient than robots programmed to do a specific task. At present, the field of Robotics has come to its spotlight with cutting-edge technologies and innovations that would result in promising growth in the upcoming years.

AI Intergrated Robots

Artificial intelligence has undoubtedly performed a significant role in automation. Branches of AI such as Neural Networks, Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing have given a new outlook for the advancement of robotics. Rather than performing a specific task, AI enables the robots to perform their functions more intelligently and productively, such as sort data and identify more efficient methods on their own, identify the need for repairs and alerting them without any human involvement, do predictive analysis on operation outputs with new advancements and much more. Incorporating cloud technologies and machine learning would allow the robots to learn from each other by sharing their knowledge which would eventually increase the efficiency and speed of deployment of robots.

Robots-as-a-service

The traditional business model that customers need to pay upfront the total cost of developing a robot has a barrier of entry for most startups and small or medium scale businesses. Providing Robotic systems as a service where the customer only needs to pay the cost of operation would become a better business solution for a company on any scale to try out and adapt to new technologies. With the companys' revenues getting a big hit from the pandemic, the pay-per-use model got colossal traction, and most of the robotic companies provided this service for their clients. Since interest in automation keeps growing exponentially, the RaaS model for robotics would be the next new trend for Robotic companies to adapt to meet the market demands. Earlier, only large companies had the potential to invest in automation, and the RaaS model would break the barricade the small-scale companies had towards automation.

Industrial Robots replacing Human Workers

A few years ago, 90% of the robots were used in the automobile industry, assembling parts or performing repetitive tasks. This has changed rapidly over the past few years. Robots are placed worldwide performing multiple tasks such as supply chain management, sewing, household activities, and even complex tasks such as providing cyber security and algorithm development. Besides tech companies, robotic technology is being heavily used in the health sector, food & beverage production, agriculture, and consumer & household sectors, which clearly shows the influence robotics and automation have on replacing monotonous and repetitive jobs requiring less intellectuality.

Embedded Vision and Machine Vision

Machine Vision employs video cameras to send the analog signals, which later converts them to digital signals and involves digital signal processing to process and send the data to a robotic system. This enables the machines to identify the objects in the real world. Operating a robot with machine vision needs a lot of energy and considerable space, which would definitely be costly due to its high performance and versatility. Embedded vision gets an edge over machine vision by providing a compact product that uses a significantly less amount of energy compared to machine vision technology. Embedded systems have a camera inbuilt to an image processor, which brings the image capturing and processing into one device. This forgoes the bulkiness and the weight of fully-fledged machine vision systems that would be useful in creating drones, movable bots and self-driving vehicles. Robotic companies are working towards developing industrial robots with machine vision systems that would be highly beneficial for the production plants to ensure the safety of workers when robots are collaborating with workers. Machine vision and embedded vision systems would enable the large warehouses to maintain their workflow smoothly, and currently, large-scale companies like Amazon incorporate this technology for their daily operations. Self-driving cars would be considered the next most giant leap in the transportation industry. Companies like Tesla, Waymo, Ford are taking the upper hand in engineering self-driving cars using embedded vision technologies.

New Robot Configourations

As Moore's law states speed and capabilities of these computational-based applications would increase over time while we pay lesser. This has allowed everyone to purchase robotic equipment according to their needs for an affordable price. The floor cleaning bot became a famous robotic invention that took the robotic products to the domestic and household market. Within the next couple of years, more robotic appliances would be created targeting households to ease day-to-day activities for a more affordable price range. We could expect to witness robots that would replace even highly intellectual jobs in the future. Google is currently working on a machine-learning robot that could create machine learning models according to customer needs. This is similar to creating robots that could create robots that would happen soon. Also, Goole company has stated once that Google search wouldn't be just typing words on a search bar in the future, but it would be more of a live conversation with a robot who would assist you in finding the exact content you are looking for. Imagine talking to a person who knows every single detail on Google and could feed you those data in real time in any format you wish. That would be just Mind-blowing.
