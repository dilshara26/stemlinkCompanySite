Transition from Traditional Education to Self- Learning.

23 Dec 2021 4 Min read

Before the outbreak....

Before covid-19 patient became the hottest topic in the city, life of a school child was pretty normal. I was in my final year of high school when Covid was striking Sri Lanka. Students used to go to school, learned 9 subjects till they finish their Ordinary level studies, then learned 3 subjects in depth till they finish our Advance level studies. But the real question stands whether we studied the topics we loved to study, did we get the opportunity for that and whether there’s a proper encouragement to study the things we love? If someone wants to dig in further, it’s fair to question whether most of the subjects we studied in both of these Ordinary level and Advance level stages really benefited for us to cater the industry needs. But then again talking about loop holes in the Education system is not the motive here in this blog and if we do so, that would not help any of us.

Here's the Silver lining

Right after the Covid outbreak children lost the opportunity of going to the schools. But the silver lining here is that children got their time to explore what they really love and what they really want to learn apart from the traditional school subjects. But then again, this argument could be debatable since not all the students in this country are fortunate enough to afford self-education neither self-driven or well-guided. But if we focus on positives, online education platforms like Coursera and edX launched multiple scholarship campaigns with the support of foreign government organizations which opened up a virtual space for students to explore their knowledge from the courses and specializations designed by the world best universities in whatever the field they wish. Consider a situation of a student who studied physical sciences for advance level and finally figures out that he loves to peruse in the field of IT. But since he was not much exposed towards disciplines like programming, he had to bid farewell for 3 years of subject content that he has learnt so hard and had to start learning something totally new in order to switch to the career path he loves to proceed. On the other hand, nowadays kids have the opportunity to escape from the traditional boundaries, term test pressure and learn programming skills, graphic designing skills or whatever kind of professional skill they prefer. So, by the end of their high schools, they would be exposing to the job market with a handful of professional skills that industry needs at a very young age and they would have a greater edge above all of us. There are lots of interesting fields that students could excel from their childhood which doesn’t require high mathematical, statistical or scientific knowledge. At the statuesque we could see numerous freely available full courses on YouTube and on other platforms which were done by highly skilled professionals to great comprehensive levels. Best example could be Freecodecamp, edureka, Treehouse, Khan Academy etc.

Escape from conventional practices

Missing the school time due to country situation is definitely tragic. But that might be the beginning of a different journey in your life. Knowledge is freely available in a cloud space and what’s needed from your end is just a device which has access to internet, a peaceful mindset and a cup of coffee. Creating Doctors, Engineers, Accountants and lawyers through an education system is great. But is it worth to sacrifice all the children’s’ lives to create a competition in order to filter the best fitting students for the above careers still stands questionable.

We as STEM link, it's our mission to direct you to a better pathway above conventional education. We are coming up with various courses to bridge you with indstrial practices.

Dilshara Hasanka

Founder of STEM link, Mathematics consultant for UK, Computer Science tutor for STEM link, Content Specialist for B Your Brand, Freelance Journalist & Software Engineering Undergraduate
